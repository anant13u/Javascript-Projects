Business Counselling & Entrepreneurship Development ACI provides sector-specific counselling to young entrepreneurs and MSMEs, offering guidance on various business opportunities. One-on-one business counselling is provided voluntarily by experienced member entrepreneurs trained in professional counselling. Additionally, ACI offers access to business ideas, government schemes, incentives, and incubation centers across India. Skill Assessment As an independent assessing body, ACI evaluates the competencies of individuals trained under the Modular Employable Skills (MES) Courses and those who have acquired skills informally. ACI is also in the final stages of developing online skill assessments, further enhancing accessibility and efficiency in evaluation. Content Development & Certification ACI conducts sector-specific studies to develop question banks under the Sector Skill Councils (SSC) and Skill Development Initiatives (SDI) schemes, ensuring rigorous assessment standards. Skill Assessment and Certifications ACI operates as an impartial, third-party organization with no involvement in training programs, which guarantees the quality and credibility of its assessments. Since 2015, ACI has assessed more than 40,000 candidates through a network of over 350 highly qualified assessors under the DGET-Modular Employable Skills (MES) Courses, including candidates who have acquired skills informally. ACI is accredited by the DGET-Modular Employable Skills (MES) under the National Accreditation Board of Education & Training, New Delhi, ensuring adherence to the highest levels of performance, integrity, and quality. With offices across most states in India, ACI maintains approximately 15-20 accredited examiners per state, operating within a well-defined framework to meet local and regional requirements. National Skill Qualification Framework (NSQF) The National Skill Qualification Framework (NSQF) is a quality assurance framework that organizes qualifications according to levels of knowledge, skills, and aptitude. Comprising ten levels, each represents a different level of complexity and expertise. National Occupation Standards (NOS): These standards define the measurable performance outcomes expected from individuals in specific tasks, serving as benchmarks for various education and training programs. Assessments: ACI’s assessments focus on: ◦ Competency-Based Assessment: Collecting evidence and making judgments about whether competency has been achieved, confirming an individual’s ability to perform to workplace standards. ◦ Performance-Based Assessment: Measuring individual performance in the workplace. ◦ Student Learning Evidence: Demonstrating the extent to which students have met stated goals or objectives. ACI's assessment approach emphasizes transparency, accountability, authenticity, validity, reliability, flexibility, and fairness, utilizing a range of tools and tasks tailored to occupational competencies and embracing an inclusive approach.
